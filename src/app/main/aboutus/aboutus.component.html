PROJECT PRAISE


Program in Reading Advocacy Intended for Students' Enrichment in Reading Comprehension –is a Computer-Based Strategic Intervention Material (SIM) for Students under Frustration Level which helps to improve the reading level of learners from frustration to instructional and/or independent.

Learning to read is a particularly hard endeavor for students. There are things to consider such as phonemic awareness; phonics, spelling pattern, vocabulary, comprehension, etc., These things can make reading frustrating for Senior High school students. Thus, we recognize that our school is faced with variety of challenges when it comes to teaching reading. To address the reading problems and to give assistance to learners with reading difficulties, the School Reading Coordinator devised a reading program which aims to assess the learners’ reading ability. Helping students with reading problems is an on-going process. Helping students who are struggling with reading begins with understanding the difficulties that are hindering them. Through various reading materials, instructional devices, reading strategies, and techniques, we can help improve the deteriorating reading proficiency of our learners nowadays. This assessment led to the presentation of the School Reading Program.

This project is proposed to achieve the following objectives:

  1. Contextualize a computer - based strategic intervention material for students at-risk;

  2. Offer fun, stimulating and hands-on activities that will augment students' skills in reading comprehension;

  3. Assist students in the use of computer-based SIM

  4. Improve their reading level from frustration to instructional and/or independent level.


II. VISION A 21st century environment that promotes learning through literacy that leads to high academic achievement, independent reading and learning, and collaboration of school administrators, teachers, students, and stakeholders.

III. MISSION To provide targeted reading program and interventions which enable Senior High school students to become independent readers and lifelong learners.
